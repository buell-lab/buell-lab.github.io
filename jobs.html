Postdoctoral Fellowship Position in Plant Evolutionary Genomics
Position Summary: The The Buell Lab joined the University of Georgia Department of Crop & Soil Sciences and the Center for Applied Genetic Technologies in August 2021 and has an opening for a Postdoctoral Research Associate. This Post-Doctoral Research Associate in the Buell lab will be responsible investigating the evolutionary origins of tuber formation as part of an NSF-funded project. The Post-Doctoral Research associate will be responsible for bioinformatic analyses, data interpretation, functional validation of candidate loci, and manuscript preparation. The Post-Doctoral Associate will also oversee undergraduate students who will generate constructs, perform plant care, isolate nucleic acids, and transform plants.

Ample opportunities for interactions and collaborations with bioinformaticists, genomicists, geneticists, and breeders exist within the Buell lab that will provide broad exposure to disciplines relevant in plant breeding, genetics, genomics, and computational biology. The Buell lab provides a robust training environment for postdoctoral associates seeking careers in academia and industry and applicants interested in a dynamic, positive research experience are especially encouraged to apply.

UGA provides competitive salary and excellent benefits for postdoctoral associates. Starting salary will be $47,500. The position is a one-year initial appointment with potential extension for two additional years. Desired start date is September 2021.

Desired Qualifications: Competitive candidates will be broadly trained in genetics, genomics, bioinformatics, molecular biology, and biology. A research background in the plant sciences with a publication record showing productivity is preferred. Demonstrated leadership in research desired. Demonstrated critical assessment skills essential. Ability to communicate and demonstrate in-depth knowledge on subject matter is required. Strong inter-personal and team work skills required. Proficiency in gene editing, molecular biology, genomics, and/or bioinformatics required.
