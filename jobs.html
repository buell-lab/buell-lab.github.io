Postdoctoral Fellowship Position in Tuber Genome Biology
Position Summary: The Buell Lab (https://buell-lab.github.io/) at the University of Georgia’s Institute of Plant Breeding, Genetics, & Genomics has an opening for a Postdoctoral Research Associate. The Post-Doctoral Research Associate will be responsible for exploring tuber genome biology using single cell multi-omics, comparative genomics, bioinformatics and functional genomics in potato as well as other tuber-producing species. We have a multitude of exciting research foci such as the regulatory landscape of tuber initiation, evolutionary origins of tuber formation, and the mechanisms by which intra-genome variation impact tuber phenotypes, all projects with exceptional potential to answer long-standing questions in plant biology as well as impact crop productivity.
Ample opportunities for interactions and collaborations with bioinformaticists, genomicists, geneticists, and breeders exist within the Buell lab that will provide broad exposure to disciplines relevant in plant biology, breeding, genetics, genomics, and computational biology. The Buell lab provides a robust training environment for postdoctoral associates seeking careers in academia and industry and applicants interested in a dynamic, positive research experience are especially encouraged to apply. UGA provides competitive salary and excellent benefits for postdoctoral associates. Starting salary will be $52,500. The position is a one-year initial appointment with potential extension for two additional years. Desired start date is May 2023. Desired Qualifications: Competitive candidates will be broadly trained in genetics, genomics, bioinformatics, molecular biology, and biology. A research background in the plant sciences with a publication record showing productivity is required. Demonstrated leadership in research required. Demonstrated critical assessment skills required. Ability to communicate and demonstrate in-depth knowledge on subject matter is required. Strong inter-personal and team work skills required. Proficiency in gene editing, molecular biology, genomics, and/or bioinformatics required.
Required Degree: Doctorate – in genetics, genomics, bioinformatics, molecular biology, plant biology, biology or related field.
Required Application Materials: Please submit a CV, a 2-page statement of research experience and interest to Robin.Buell@uga.edu. You must also apply for the position at the UGA Job Posting Site (https://www.ugajobsearch.com/session/new) under posting XXXX.
Special Instructions: For more information about the position, email Robin.Buell@uga.edu
