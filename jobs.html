Student Research Opportunities Summer 2024

The Buell lab in the Center for Applied Genetic Technologies is looking for undergraduate students interested in hands-on research-for-credit experience in plant molecular biology, genetics, and genomics.

Students will be assigned to one of two projects:


(1) Biotechnology approaches to synthesis of pharmacologically relevant molecules from medicinal plant species
(2) Engineering a minimal plant genome as a chassis for the bioeconomy

Research activities include but are not limited to:
(1) Optimizing and preparing samples (protoplast or nuclei) for single cell genomics experiments on medicinal plants (Figures A-B).
(2) Nuclei acid (DNA and RNA) isolation from various plant species (Figure C).
(3) Building plasmids to test gene function.
(4) Testing gene function in plants using stable (Figure D) or transient transformation (Figure E) or gene editing (Figure G-H).
(5) Plant care and propagation (Figure F).

Students must enroll for 4 credits (~12 hours/week), of which, there is a minimum of three 4-hour blocks of effort per week required.

Candidates must be interested in plant biology research, proactive, self-motivated, and have a sense of intellectual curiosity to be invested in their research. Previous laboratory experience is not required. Relevant coursework on genetics, biochemistry, and/or plant biology is preferred but not required. This training position is an excellent opportunity for undergraduates interested in graduate studies in biological sciences and/or a career in the biotech industry.

To apply, email unofficial transcript, a cover letter, a CV/resume, and weekly availability between May 14-July 31 to Dr. Robin Buell (robin.buell@uga.edu) by April 15, 2024.
