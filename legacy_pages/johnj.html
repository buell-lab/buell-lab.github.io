John Johnston

I joined the Buell Lab in 2013 after 4 years working for the MSU High Performance Computing Center and the Institute for Cyber-Enabled Research. For the past 12 years I have specialized in providing high-performance computing solutions and bioinformatics assistance for life science researchers. In a previous life I was a hydrogeologist who worked on remedial investigations, risk assessments and remedial actions for groundwater, surface water, and soil contamination problems. I'm a registered Professional Geologist. In my spare time I enjoy travel, dining out, building lamps, and tinkering with watches, computers, and anything else mechanical or electrical. My dog Lulu seems to think she's French.
